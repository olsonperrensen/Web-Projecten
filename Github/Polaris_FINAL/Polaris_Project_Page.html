ABOUT ME

Based on continuous interest on computers, I Maximiliano Iturria Cardozo (born August 30 - 2000) am the one behind the scenes. I will be offering you an online or alternative solution for playing audio files. My field of study is Artificial Intelligence and I currently am in the second semester. The availability of an audio player (with features out of the conventional) freely available to anyone with internet access makes it possible for possible educational partners to use Polaris as a help tool in these efforts. Students are encouraged to try the Demonstration page, along with the F.A.Q (or Frequently Asked Questions) with questions regarding the status of the audio player, visualizer, spectogram and other additional features. I wish you a pleaseant experience.

PROPEL YOUR LIFE

You're a dreamer. But also a doer. And now you're considering investing a part of your day to try the antidote of Spotify. Polais is a great place to do what you have to do, play audio files and see how they would look like based on a series of visualizations. If you are unexposed to spectograms, this may seem completely new to you. I have designed Polaris to fit your busy life, offering evening, weekend, and any-time playability for many of the commonly supported audio files.

HOW POLARIS WAS MADE

Basically it is a combination of what every modern webpage is made out of, and that is HTML for the structural part of it, CSS for the styling part of it, and JavaScript for the interactive `real-time` part of it. I have incorporated an additional tool in order for JavaScript to react to audio more cohesively, and that is a Framework called P5.js
